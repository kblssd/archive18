Networking Concepts (23%):

OSI Model:

Layer 1: Physical layer

bits in a wire (Hub, Repeater, Network cabling)

Layer 2: Data Link

physical addressing (MAC addresses(48-bit), Bridge, Switch, NIC(Network adapter), ARP, Frames)

Layer 3: Network

path determination (Routers, IP, ICMP, Packets)

Layer 4: Transport

TCP(Segments) = reliable/connection-oriented, UDP(Datagrams) = fast/connectionless & ports

Layer 5: Session

managing connections (opening, closing, and maintaining connections)

Layer 6: Presentation

data translation (data compression, and data encryption/decryption)

Layer 7: Application

user interface (direct interaction between the end-user and the network & HTTP, FTP, SMTP)

Botany

All plants need moisture, nutrients, sunlight, and a suitable temperature. Water is vital for plants since it is used in photosynthesis and to keep leaves and stems firm and upright. Plants have a variety of methods to absorb and store it. Most take up moisture through microscopic root hairs and lose it via the leaves in a process called transpiration. Plants are classified according to hardiness: tender specimens tolerate temperatures down to 41ºF (5ºC), frost-hardy to 23ºF (-5ºC), and fully hardy to -4ºF (-20ºC). Every plant has specific temperature requirements; young plants are especially vulnerable and usually need protection to survive. Seedlings that have been raised indoors should be gradually introduced to cooler outdoor temperatures, a process known as "hardening off." Frost-sensitive plants should be wrapped up in situ or moved to a frost-free location over winter. Tender, exotic plants, such as cacti, aloes, and agaves, require a warm, dry site and need protection against frost in cold winter climates

Summary:

Watering: Absorbed via root hairs; lost through transpiration; keeps stems firm for photosynthesis.

Hardiness Categories:
Tender: 5 Celsius
Frost-hardy: -5 Celsius
Fully hardy: -20 Celsius

Temperature Management: Protect young plants and use "hardening off" to transition seedlings outdoors.

Winter Protection: Wrap frost-sensitive plants or move them to frost-free locations; exotics (cacti, aloes) require warm, dry sites.
